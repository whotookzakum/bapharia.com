Liquid Memories

Liquid memories are utility buffs that can be activated at a liquid memory stand.

Increases chance of higher starting level when crafting weapons

Increases exp when leveling your weapon

Increases gathering speed

Increases enemy drop rate

Discounted prices in npc shops

How to use

Unlock each color through side quests--there are 2 vials of each color
Check the world map for their locations

Fill empty vials by doing actions related to the memory, or waiting for time to pass

You can pay to drink the collected memories at a liquid memory stand
Drinking two of the same color results in a longer effect duration
You cannot drink a color that is currently in effect

*Effect values will be added when more data is available

Combat

A brief overview of the combat components in blue protocol.

Party vs Party

Player Party vs Enemy AI Party is a system where enemy mobs will also cooperate to try and take down you and your allies.

Drops and EXP Distribution

All parties with a member that dealth at least 1 damage to a monster will receive full EXP and drops.

Stamina

Stamina will be consumed when sprinting or evading while within an enemy's aggro range.
Evading can be used to cancel many attack animations.

Elements

Enemies can have elemental weaknesses. Read more on the elements page.

Buffs

Status ailments that can apply to players or enemies. Read more on the buffs page.

Skills

You can assign 4 tactical skills, 3 tactical abilities, 1 ultimate skill, and any amount of permanent abilities.
View these skills on the skill builder pages for each class. Developers prefer adding more skills rather than more classes.

Mechanics

Assault Attack Melee classes receive a damage and enemy knockdown bonus when dropping onto enemies from high ground.
Dodge Attack Attacking right after dodging will allow a seamless transition into the attack animation.
Perfect Dodge Dodging with excellent timing will grant you class-unique bonuses and buffs indicated by a blue particle effect.
Elevation Bonus Ranged classes receive a damage bonus when attacking enemies from high ground.

Currently no optimal skill rotations are known, so just spam skills off cooldown.

Elements

Elemental attacks apply abnormal statuses on targets and boost damage output. They can be utilized through skills and weapons with elements.

System Explanation

Using elemental attacks on an enemy causes elemental charge to accumulate.
When the elemental charge exceeds a certain amount, the abnormal status effect of the most charged element will be applied.
Attacking with different elements will still build elemental charge.
Level 1~2 applies abnormal status effects; as the level increases so does the effect. Level 3 is a burst effect.

Fusion

Fusion is the act of attaching battle imagine to your empty weapon slots.

How to fuse

Eligible weapons will have 1~4 empty slots. Speak to the Weapon Reconstructor.
The target battle imagine will be consumed and its ability will be applied to your weapon with a random value.

Notes

A battle imagine's ability is randomly selected from a list of possible abilities.

The effectiveness value shown is the 3 star value.
This is the value you will get if you equip the battle imagine to your character.
It will be randomized within its category when fused to a weapon.

Currently there is no way to remove fused battle imagine from weapons.

Reduce cooldown (ultimate) formula

This ability is applied as a percentage. Formula courtesy of Baskmedia

( CD - TA ) * ( 100 - IA ) / 100 = cooldown

CD: cooldown of ultimate (default 60s)   |    TA: Reduce ult cooldown Tactical Abilitiy (at level 3, 7 sec)   |    IA: Imagine ability effectiveness (fused or equipped)

Progression Guide

This guide is to quickly get through the game contents. Please enjoy the game however you like.
Will most likely change when new story quests are added and AR requirements are higher.
Information is current as of CBT.

Lv 1~10: Main Story Quest

Fight some Elite Monsters to overlevel and grab mount imagine ASAP (req. lv 8; Minsterhorn NPC).
Grab any nearby warp gates and gather materials for the later quests during your travels.
Advanced players should farm materials for weapons/imagine along the way as well.

Lv 10~30: Arena

Around level 10-15, craft the fire weapon.
This dungeon is the shortest and requires the least runs, and material locations overlap with story quests.
Craft the 2 battle imagine that you have the most materials for to fill up your skill slots.

Lv 30~35: Valley of Machines

At level 35, craft the earth weapon.
The materials take some time so doing this after lv35 is recommended.

Complete your loadout

Gather battle imagine for combat, inner imagine, then battle imagine for fusion.

    Potentially the best fusion lines -- not yet tested
  • Damage Increase (melee/ranged, normal attacks, ultimate, tactical 1)
  • Reduce Cooldown (ultimate)
  • ATK
  • Crit rate
  • Crit damage
  • Stamina Increase*

*An animation cancel meta might prefer stamina

The game cycle is endlessly crafting in hopes of a god weapon (0.00272% or lower)
